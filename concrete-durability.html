Freeze-Thaw scaling of concrete per RILEM CIF

Freeze-thaw (F-T) scaling per RILEM CIF setup is illutrated as follows. The sample after cutting is sealed on all lateral surfaces using butyl tape. One open surface is then exposed to pure water for presaturation prior to the F-T test. After presaturation, the pure water is replace by the test solution, typically a NaCl solution. The specimen is subsequently placed in a F-T chamber , where the temperature is cycled between 20 degree Celcius to -20 degree Celcius before returning to 20 degree Celcius. Every 24 hours corresponds to two complete F-T cycles.

Typical results are shown for specimens exposed to 3% NaCl solution (frost–salt attack) and 0% NaCl solution (pure frost attack). The concrete mixtures in both cases are identical, consisting of adequately air-entrained concrete with a water-to-binder (W/B) ratio of 0.42 and 25% slag as a cement replacement. Scaling mass loss and mass gain due to cryogenic suction are typically evaluated after 14 freeze–thaw cycles.
